# Jun-Sik Yoo

**Computational physicist and empirical AI researcher working on high-performance scientific computing, lattice QCD, and transformer model internals.**

I study complex computational systems through large-scale numerical experiments. My background is in lattice QCD, high-performance scientific computing, and precision calculations in particle and nuclear physics. I am now extending this experience toward empirical AI research, with a focus on transformer internal dynamics, interpretability, quantization diagnostics, and scalable model-analysis pipelines.

## Research Interests

- **AI model internals and interpretability**  
  Layer-wise transformer dynamics, token-token interaction structure, activation diagnostics, and internal metrics for model behavior.

- **High-performance scientific computing**  
  Large-scale numerical workflows, GPU/CPU optimization, distributed computation, and reproducible analysis pipelines.

- **Lattice QCD and particle physics**  
  First-principles calculations of hadronic matrix elements, proton decay, electroweak radiative corrections, nucleon structure, and CP-violating operators.

## Selected Research Directions

### Transformer layer-update geometry

I analyze transformer computation at the level of layer updates and representation-space geometry. The goal is to develop diagnostics that reveal how internal computation changes across layers, model families, prompts, quantization levels, and interventions.

### Quantization diagnostics for internal computation

Low-bit quantization is usually evaluated through output-level metrics such as perplexity, accuracy, or KL divergence. I am interested in complementary internal diagnostics: which components of the layer computation are preserved, distorted, or selectively erased by quantization?

### Lattice QCD at physical pion mass

My physics work includes first-principles lattice-QCD calculations of proton decay matrix elements at the physical pion mass, reducing previously uncontrolled systematic uncertainty from chiral extrapolation. This work contributes to quantitative constraints on grand unified theories and proton lifetime predictions.

### Electroweak radiative corrections

I have worked on lattice-QCD calculations of electroweak box-diagram contributions relevant to precision beta decay and CKM unitarity tests.
